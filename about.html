Mission Statement

Our mission is to empower individuals through coding education and collaborative projects, with a strong focus on securing internships at top FAANG companies. We aim to develop real-world skills and provide a supportive environment for growth.

History of the Club

Founded in 2024, Pelican Code was established to address the competitive nature of the software engineering job market. Recognizing the challenges, our founders created this club to help students navigate the industry, focusing on internship opportunities and hands-on projects.

Achievements and Milestones

Our club has successfully helped numerous members secure internships and jobs at top tech companies. We've hosted various events, guest speaker sessions, and completed several impactful projects.

Club Structure and Bylaws

Our club is structured to provide equal opportunities for all members to contribute and grow. We operate with a clear set of bylaws that ensure transparency and fairness in our decision-making processes.

Objective: Secure members internships to top FAANG companies by developing real-world skills and providing a collaborative environment.

Voting and Amendments: Any club member can propose amendments, which require a two-thirds majority vote by the Executive Board and ratification by the General Body. The President holds the executive power to override amendments.

Membership: Open to all students at Tulane and Loyola, regardless of their major. Membership is categorized into General and Project members, each with specific attendance and voting rights.
